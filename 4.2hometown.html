Welcome to Bihar! My Hometown

Bihar, located in eastern India, is known for its rich culture, history, and natural beauty. The state is famous for its vibrant festivals, traditional folk music and dance, and delicious cuisine like litti-chokha. Bihar is home to ancient sites such as Bodh Gaya, where Lord Buddha attained enlightenment, and Nalanda, one of the world's oldest universities. The lush green fields, the majestic Ganga river, and the beautiful heritage monuments make Bihar truly unique.

Cultural Highlights

  • Chhath Puja: A major festival dedicated to the Sun God, celebrated on the banks of rivers. Devotees offer prayers at sunrise and sunset, standing in water, and the festival is known for its purity and vibrant rituals.
  • Madhubani Art: Famous traditional paintings from the Mithila region.
  • Bhojpuri Folk Music: Energetic and soulful music reflecting the spirit of Bihar. It is performed during festivals and celebrations, featuring traditional instruments like dholak and harmonium.

Beauty of Bihar

Mahabodhi Temple, Bodh Gaya
The Mahabodhi Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site where Lord Buddha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi tree. The temple complex is a major pilgrimage site for Buddhists from around the world.

Madhubani Painting
Traditional art form from Mithila, known for its intricate patterns and vibrant colors, often depicting nature, mythology, and folklore.

Nalanda University Ruins
Nalanda was an ancient center of learning from the 5th to 12th century. It attracted scholars from all over Asia and is considered one of the first residential universities in the world.
